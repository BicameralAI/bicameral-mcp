# Consolidated Compliance Remediation Audit — bicameral-mcp

**Audit date:** 2026-06-03
**Prepared for:** Workstreet SOC 2 / ISO delivery team (kickoff 2026-06-09)
**System under audit:** `bicameral-mcp` — a local Model Context Protocol (MCP) server that ingests engineering decisions from agents/integrations, grounds them to code symbols via a SurrealDB append-only ledger + tree-sitter index, and serves them back to AI coding agents (caller-LLMs).
**Verification posture:** Every finding below was subjected to an adversarial second pass. Findings whose verification verdict was *refuted* have been dropped; *adjusted* findings carry the corrected status/severity. Statuses are grounded in cited repository evidence; nothing is asserted as met or gapped without a checked file:line.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

bicameral-mcp presents an **engineering-led control environment that is genuinely strong on the technical/supply-chain plane and deliberately scoped on the deployment plane**, with a small set of real, mostly self-declared gaps concentrated in **privacy/PII disposal**, **injection hardening of the ledger query path**, and **organizational policy artifacts that conventionally live in the Workstreet/Vanta layer rather than in-repo**.

The system's strongest assets — which an auditor should credit immediately — are: a fail-closed Sigstore/cosign keyless supply-chain pipeline with Rekor attestation and install-time signature verification; a Merkle-sealed append-only governance ledger (META_LEDGER); deterministic, server-enforced ingest gates (size cap, rate limit, prompt-injection canary, secret/PHI/PAN detect-and-refuse); a structured no-PII audit log; and a documented, load-bearing single-tenant trust boundary.

The most material residual risks are: (1) **SurrealQL injection** on the caller-LLM `bind` path where older read-path query helpers interpolate record IDs without the existing validator (OWASP LLM07 / A03); (2) **incomplete PII disposal** — raw operator/third-party PII persists inline in `decision.speakers`/`source_ref`, in the 30-day changefeed, and in team-mode event journals, with no shipped erase-subject CLI (SOC2 C1.x / GDPR Art.17); (3) **no lawful-basis/consent surface for ingesting third-party PII** (GDPR Art.6/7); and (4) **incomplete GitHub Actions SHA-pinning**, including an unpinned `trufflehog@main` ref in the very workflow meant to scan for secrets (SOC2 CC8.1 / OWASP LLM05·A06 / SSDF PO.5).

### EU AI Act risk classification

**Limited-risk.** bicameral-mcp performs no Annex III high-risk use case (no biometrics, employment, credit, critical-infrastructure, or law-enforcement decisioning), does not ship or train a general-purpose AI model (Art.51 GPAI does not attach), and does not generate synthetic content passed off as authentic (Art.50(2) deepfake provisions do not apply). The binding obligation is **Art.50 transparency**; Arts.9/10/12/14/15 are voluntary/best-practice alignment for this system, not legally-binding high-risk obligations. A customer's *downstream* use may be high-risk; that escalation is tracked via a `high_risk_target` plan flag (see Art.9/MAP findings). Evidence: `docs/governance/compliance-stance-matrix.md:23`.

## 2. Scope & Methodology

**Frameworks covered:** SOC 2 (CC1–CC9, Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, Privacy); OWASP (Web Top 10 2021 + LLM Top 10); NIST AI RMF (GOVERN/MAP/MEASURE/MANAGE) and NIST SSDF (SP 800-218); GDPR; EU AI Act.

**Method:** Each control was assessed against actual repository files (Read/Grep/Glob) with cited `path:line` evidence. Every finding then underwent an **adversarial verification pass** that actively attempted to refute it. Verdicts: *confirmed* (finding stands), *adjusted* (status or severity corrected), *refuted* (dropped). This report reflects the post-verification truth set.

**Refuted/dropped during verification:**
- **NIST SSDF PW.4** — refuted to *met/low*. The flagged lockfile gap is a formally adopted, adjudicated floor-only stance (`docs/policies/install-trust-model.md:9-45`, closing OWASP-03), backed by SBOM + Rekor attestation; the brief itself downgraded it to a posture note. Reported in the Appendix as MET, not as a gap.

**Adjusted during verification (selected):** GDPR Art.6/7 escalated *partial → gap* (no consent surface for the at-risk processing); SOC2 Availability *partial → met* (`docs/sla.md`, `docs/policies/ledger-export.md` close the cited gaps); OWASP A10 SSRF *partial → met* (all outbound hosts verified non-caller-controlled); OWASP LLM01 *medium → low* (output-path fencing in `pulse/render.py` exists). Severity down-adjustments applied to NIST GOVERN-1.2/MAP-1.1/Art.9 (real enforcement or high-risk-only scope), GDPR Art.30/Art.33-34 (operator-layer, P3 self-rated), and EU AI Act Art.10 (high-risk-only article applied to a limited-risk system).

**NOT covered by this audit:** (a) Offensive validation — the imminent penetration test covers exploit confirmation separately; this report identifies the SurrealQL injection *surface* but does not weaponize it. (b) The Workstreet/Vanta organizational policy plane (HR, org chart, background checks, ethics attestations, vendor DPAs, enterprise risk register, incident-response program) — repeatedly flagged below as the external policy layer the repo cannot itself contain. (c) GitHub server-side settings (branch protection, required status checks) — not verifiable from the repo; an auditor must export them from org config.
